Bhit shah

Bhit or Bhit Shah is a small town located in Matiari District, Sindh, Pakistan. The town is best known as the location of the shrine to the Sindhi Sufi poet, Shah Abdul Latif Bhittai, who came to be called Bhittai on account of the town's name.

Rani Bagh

Rani Bagh is a fascinating subplot in the history of Mumbai. It was established at Byculla in 1861 by the Agri-Horticultural Society of Western India as Victoria Gardens and in 1890, a small zoo was added to it. Long before iconic heritage structures like the Victoria Terminus and Gateway of India, there was Rani Bagh.

Sindh Museum

The Sindh Museum is a museum located in Hyderabad, Sindh, Pakistan. The museum was established in 1971 to collect, preserve, study, and exhibit the records of the cultural history of Sindh. It also sometimes hosts cultural fairs.
